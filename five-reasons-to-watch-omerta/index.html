Five Reasons to Watch Omerta

On the cold winter evening of December 24, 1999, Indian Airline Flight 814 left the Kathmandu International Airport for New Delhi - but never made it. The plane was hijacked by mujahideen men who ordered the flight to stop in Amritsar, Lahore, Dubai and finally in Taliban controlled Kandahar, Afghanistan. The hijackers held the passengers hostage for 7 days while negotiating the release of three men -

Mushtaq Ahmed Zargar

Maulana Masood Azhar

and Ahmed Omar Saeed Sheikh - The namesake protagonist of the upcoming movie Omerta.

Omerta is publicized by director hansal mehta as a study of evil in human nature. Whether a blockbuster or not, we think this is a movie worth watching. Here are the 5 reasons why we think Omerta is worth your time.

1. Rajkummar Rao: RR has established a league of his own by establishing himself as the uncrowned ruler of small yet powerful roles. When cast as the lead, Rao immerses the audience in the realism of his characters and not the pomp of stardom. While 2017 was a sour lemon for bollywood, Rao remained a sight for sore eyes everytime he took to the screen. Well, almost everytime. Irrespective of the performance of the movie, Rajkummar Rao is always a pleasure to watch.

2. Hansal Mehta: Omerta is directed by Hansal Mehta, who began his career with the housewife hearthrob Sanjeev Kapoor on Khana Khazana. Pursuing darker subjects since, Hansal Mehta struck gold with Shahid which bagged the national award for best movie in 2013. Since then he has consistently given heart wrenching movies like city lights - a modern day do bhiga zameen , aligarh - the lost story of untold cruelties and simran - a unique and quirky movie.
Three of his most brilliant movies have been with Rao, why should this be an exception?

3. Biopic failure: Biopics are often botched in bollywood, with the stardom of the actor consuming the real person in the story. Our preference to watch our whites and blacks as starkly different remove the humanity from biopics by larger than life potrayl and an avoidance of the grey area. Hansal Mehta's handling of Shahid with an unflinching eye gives one hope that this movie will be abut story first and narratives later.

4. Hijacking incident of 1999: Acts of terrorism are far from a popular topic for movie making. The intense stories that develop in mere hours of such events often never reach the population of India. This movie is a reminder of a dark time in our history which may be fading from our memory now. The event that unfolded in the seven days of negotiation are sure worth a revision.

5. The Jury is out: The story of Omar Saeed is not one that has concluded. Saeed is not a boy risen form the dust of the hindu kush. He has received education at the most elite institutions of Pakistan and is a drop out of London School of Economics. He is currently held in a Pakistani prison awaiting trial.

The governments of state that follow laws prosecute terrorists with due process and time. Meanwhile, the perpetrators of anarchy bide their time holding their vow silence - what the mobs called - Omerta.
